# Stop Trigger

The stop trigger is available to any person affected by a registered authority. It creates a permanent, public, timestamped record that a concern has been raised and requires a documented response.

---

## What This Does

Filing a stop trigger does not immediately halt any authority. It does three things:

- Creates a permanent public record that you raised a specific concern about a specific authority on a specific date
- Notifies the authority that a stop trigger has been filed against it
- Starts a response clock — the authority must respond within 30 days or the non-response itself becomes a finding in the public record

The mechanism operators know the record is public. Their response — or failure to respond — is also public. That visibility is the mechanism. Silence is not an option.

---

## Who Can File

Anyone who is affected by a registered authority. You do not need to be a lawyer, an expert, or a formal party. If a registered authority is doing something to you or people around you, you can file.

Good-faith filing may not be penalized. If an authority retaliates against you for filing a stop trigger, that retaliation is itself a constitutional violation under Rule R33.

---

---

## What Happens After Filing

**Immediately:** The submission becomes a public GitHub Issue. The record exists. It cannot be deleted.

**Within 7 days:** The Bounded Power Foundation logs the trigger against the Mechanism Record in the registry and notifies the authority.

**Within 30 days:** The authority must respond publicly. Acceptable responses are:

- Acknowledgment and explanation
- Evidence of compliance
- Initiation of internal review
- Request for clarification

Non-response within 30 days is logged as a finding and added permanently to the Mechanism Record.

**After review:** If the trigger raises credible evidence of a threshold breach or constitutional violation, it is escalated to the Testing Protocol for formal evaluation.

---

## Aggregate Triggers

If 10 or more independent stop triggers are filed against the same authority within any 90-day period, an automatic aggregation review is initiated regardless of the individual trigger classifications.

---

## Filing Against an Unregistered Authority

If the authority you are concerned about is not in the registry, you can still file. Include as much identifying information as you have. The filing creates a public record that the authority exists and is operating without a registered scope, thresholds, or stop paths — which is itself a finding.
