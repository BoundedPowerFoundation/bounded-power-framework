# SCBP-05 — Testing Protocol

*Diagnostic Layer*
*Subordinate to: the Structural Constitution*

---

## Purpose

This document defines the diagnostic procedures used to evaluate whether a system exercising authority complies with the Structural Constitution for Bounded Power.

The protocol is diagnostic, not administrative. It defines evaluation criteria and classification of outcomes. It does not create institutions or perform enforcement. Its purpose is to determine what authority exists, how it operates, and whether it remains within defined constraints.

---

## Diagnostic Philosophy

Authority systems tend to fail through gradual expansion, reinterpretation, aggregation, and structural entrenchment rather than sudden violation.

Evaluation therefore focuses on:

- scope containment
- metric completeness
- outcome alignment
- ability to stop
- reversibility
- auditability
- renewal legitimacy
- resistance to fragmentation

Unknown, unobservable, or unbounded conditions are treated as failure.

---

## Entry Conditions

If authority cannot be identified, or boundaries, metrics, or outcomes cannot be determined, the system must enter provisional pause until clarity is restored.

---

## Evaluation Sequence

Each evaluation proceeds through twelve steps in order.

### Step 1 — Authority Identification

The evaluator must identify the authority being exercised by function, not by name, structure, or declared classification.

Any system that influences decisions, allocations, restrictions, or future conditions is treated as authority.

### Step 2 — Structural Boundary Mapping

The evaluator must define:

- scope
- reach
- duration
- enforcement mechanisms
- inputs and outputs
- data systems
- dependencies

If boundaries are not auditable, the system fails evaluation and must pause.

### Step 3 — Metric Completeness Evaluation

The evaluator must determine whether declared metrics, variables, signals, classifications, and inputs fully account for observed system behavior.

If observed outcomes cannot be explained by the declared metric set, the system is presumed to contain undeclared influence. Undeclared influence constitutes non-compliance.

### Step 4 — Outcome Alignment Evaluation

The evaluator must determine whether observable outcomes remain within authorized scope.

If the practical effects of a system exceed its authorized scope, the authority is deemed expanded regardless of structure, labeling, or intermediary systems. Outcome-based expansion constitutes non-compliance.

### Step 5 — Drift Detection

The evaluator must determine whether current authority exceeds original authorization in scope, duration, reach, or effect.

If drift is detected: contract to original scope, or enter renewal review.

### Step 6 — Dependency and Fragmentation Detection

The evaluator must identify whether authority is distributed across multiple entities, systems, or processes.

If multiple components function as a coordinated system, they are evaluated as a single authority. Fragmentation may not be used to evade constraints.

### Step 7 — Stop Verification

A compliant authority must remain interruptible.

If stopping the system requires permission from the system itself, or if stop mechanisms are degraded, delayed, or obstructed, the system fails the stop test.

### Step 8 — Reversibility Evaluation

The system must be capable of halt, rollback, replacement, and dismantling. If these conditions cannot be met within a defined timeframe, the authority is structurally unsafe.

### Step 9 — Auditability Assessment

All elements required to evaluate authority must be observable, verifiable, and reproducible. If evaluation cannot be performed independently, the system fails auditability.

### Step 10 — Renewal Eligibility Assessment

Authority must demonstrate:

- continued necessity
- proportional scope
- reversibility
- stop capability
- compliance with constitutional constraints

Failure results in expiration.

### Step 11 — Evidence Review

Evaluation must rely on observable evidence. If evidence is incomplete, inconsistent, or unavailable, interpretation defaults to the narrowest scope of authority. Persistent evidence failure constitutes non-compliance.

### Step 12 — Diagnostic Outcome Classification

Each evaluation must result in one or more of the following classifications:

| Classification | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Boundary Failure | Scope, reach, or duration exceeds authorization |
| Metric Failure | Declared metrics do not explain observed outcomes |
| Outcome Failure | Practical effects exceed authorized scope |
| Dependency Failure | Dependency concentration compromises stop-ability |
| Stop Failure | System cannot be interrupted independently |
| Reversibility Failure | System cannot be rolled back or dismantled |
| Auditability Failure | System cannot be independently evaluated |
| Renewal Failure | Insufficient evidence for continued authorization |

These classifications are passed to the Enforcement System.
