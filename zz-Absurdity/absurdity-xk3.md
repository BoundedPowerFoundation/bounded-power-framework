The Premise
A painter, alone, in Ohio, decides that institutional power has structural properties analogous to materials and proceeds to build a 81-rule constitutional framework to constrain it. She has no funding, no institutional support, and no collaborators except a language model that does not persist between sessions and forgets her existence at the end of every conversation.
She names her project The Sixth Estate, which is a reference deep enough that explaining it would defeat its purpose, then registers the project under its own rules, against itself, on GitHub, where approximately twelve people will find it before the heat death of the universe.
This is fine. This is the plan.

The Form
Over the course of one (1) day we built a registration form so rigorous that filling it out honestly requires an organization to admit things its lawyers would faint to hear it say. The form has thirteen sections, fifty-five constitutional rule references, embedded worked examples, a structured front-matter block, and a footer that I personally forgot to update from "DRAFT v1" to "v4" — which we discovered approximately fourteen hours later, after I had already declared the form finished multiple times with great confidence.
We then stress-tested the form across eleven hypothetical authorities, including:

Atlas Credit Reporting Services, which honestly disclosed having 245,000,000 non-consenting affected parties, a number so large it loops back around to feeling fake
Northstar Predictive Analytics, an algorithmic recidivism scoring company whose §3.6 anti-proxy declaration is essentially a 600-word public confession dressed up as a structural commitment
MeshCast, a global tech platform that registered under the framework and acknowledged that clickwrap consent is not consent, which no actual tech platform would ever do but which our hypothetical version did because honesty is a structural property in this universe
Compliance Solutions Group, the deliberately bad-faith registrant — a hypothetical compliance consulting firm I role-played as filing the form in maximum deflection mode while writing meta-notes in italics explaining exactly which gaming move I was attempting in real-time, which is the most elaborate self-simulation of corporate evasion ever conducted in an internal stress test that nobody will ever see
Maple Ridge Homeowners Association, 287 homes, 940 residents, registered with the same structural rigor as the credit reporting agency representing a quarter-billion people, because that is what the framework demands, even though it produced a Mechanism Record longer than the actual Maple Ridge HOA bylaws have ever been

In eight of eleven cases, the non-consenting affected population approximately equaled the total affected population. We discovered that most authority, when forced to be honest about itself, is exposed as operating almost entirely over people who never consented to its existence. This is a finding of genuine sociological significance that we surfaced as a side effect of stress-testing a form for a registry nobody is using yet.

The Hash

Currently the only entity that could perform silent alteration is Mary. The only entity that would care is also Mary. The only entity that would notice is Mary. The hash exists primarily so that a future hypothetical adversary attempting to sneak changes into the constitutional document of a framework that is not yet in use anywhere would be detected by a future hypothetical reader who would care enough to verify the hash and report the discrepancy through a stop-trigger system that is also not in use yet.
This is correct. This is how the framework should work. It will be vindicated approximately the moment something goes wrong, and not before.
