# FAQ

## Is this a political system?

No. It does not define policies or outcomes. It defines constraints on authority.

---

## Who is this for?

Anyone designing, evaluating, or interacting with systems of institutional power.

Most commonly: citizens, journalists, researchers, and civic actors who want to produce a structural analysis of an authority that affects them. Authorities themselves can also use the framework to file public self-disclosures, though they rarely do.

---

## Does this require adoption by governments?

No. It can be analyzed, adapted, or applied in parts or in full.

Citizen-produced structural diagnostics are the framework's most common use, and they do not require the authority's cooperation or any government's adoption. Anyone can produce one.

---

## Why is it written this way?

The system separates:

- human-readable explanations
- binding structural rules

This prevents interpretation from expanding authority.

---

## What makes this different?

It assumes failure and designs for containment.

Most systems assume correct behavior and attempt to correct mistakes after the fact.

## Can I use the framework without reading everything?

Yes. The [AI Registration Bundle](06-AI-Registration-Bundle.md) is a single file that contains everything an AI needs to produce a structural analysis of an authority on your behalf. Paste it into an AI chat, tell the AI which authority to analyze, and you get a downloadable result.

You don't need to read the bundle. It is for the AI to consume.

---

## Is this complete?

No system is final. This is a structured attempt to define enforceable limits on power.
