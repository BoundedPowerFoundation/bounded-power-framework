# START HERE v2

## What This Is

The Bounded Power Framework (BPF) is a system for evaluating institutions, organizations, and authorities that hold meaningful power over people.

Examples include:

- Police departments
- Courts
- Hospitals
- Employers
- School systems
- HOAs
- Regulatory agencies
- Insurance systems
- Platforms
- Nonprofits
- Financial systems

The framework helps humans and AI systems analyze whether power is:

- reviewable
- stoppable
- reversible
- accountable
- transparent
- structurally constrained

---

# What You Need

You do NOT need to be an expert.

You do NOT need legal training.

You do NOT need to understand the full framework before starting.

# Quick Start (5 Minutes)

## Step 1 — Choose an Authority

Pick one institution or authority to evaluate.

Examples:

- local police department
- hospital system
- university board
- landlord authority
- child protective services
- employer
- payment processor
- credit reporting agency

---

## Step 2 — Open an AI Tool

Use your preferred AI system.

Paste this prompt:

Help me complete a Bounded Power registration for [AUTHORITY NAME].

Repository:
https://github.com/BoundedPowerFoundation/Bounded-Power-Framework

Use the Registration Form and Data Schema from the repository.

IMPORTANT:

1. Complete the Registry Header first.
2. Ask me for missing facts one question at a time.
3. Use Unknown instead of guessing.
4. Separate facts from assumptions.
5. Apply SCBP-04 structural review before finalizing.

---

# Step 3 — Complete the Registry Header

Before writing narrative analysis, complete the top metadata fields.

Examples:

- authority name
- authority type
- location
- sector
- parent organization
- primary power held
- dependency level
- appeals process
- oversight structure

This information is important for future searchability and registry analysis.

---

# Step 4 — Perform Structural Review

After metadata is complete, evaluate:

- what powers are exercised
- who depends on the authority
- whether people can realistically opt out
- what harms can occur
- whether meaningful remedies exist
- whether oversight is independent
- whether authority can expand without review

---

# Step 5 — Apply SCBP-04

All registrations should include structural constitutional review using:

01-Framework/SCBP-04-Structural-Constitution-v1.3.md

Focus especially on:

- reversibility
- rollback
- transparency
- dependency risk
- independent review
- nonparticipant protection
- expansion by reinterpretation
- unplug-ability

# Important Rules

1. Use facts whenever possible.
2. Mark Unknown instead of inventing data.
3. Separate evidence from inference.
4. Be fair even when critical.
5. Structural analysis matters more than rhetoric.

---

# Goal

The goal is not ideological agreement.

The goal is to help humans evaluate whether systems of power remain structurally bounded, accountable, reviewable, and reversible.
