# Start Here

## What this is

The Bounded Power Framework is a public framework anyone can use to analyze institutions and authorities that hold power over people.

Examples include:

- Police departments
- Courts and prosecutors
- Hospitals and health insurers
- Schools, universities, and family services
- Employers
- Homeowners' associations
- Regulatory agencies
- Digital platforms and payment processors
- Credit-reporting agencies and tenant-screening vendors
- Nonprofits, religious institutions, and voluntary associations

The framework asks one question of any such authority:

> **Is this authority structured to remain stoppable, correctable, and bounded — or has it grown past the point where it can still be changed?**

Using the framework produces a **Mechanism Record** — a structured public document that surfaces an authority's scope, harms, dependencies, stop paths, and oversight gaps in a way most disclosure regimes never force into one place.

---

## What you need

- You do **not** need to be an expert.
- You do **not** need legal training.
- You do **not** need to understand the full framework before starting.
- AI can assist you.

The framework is built to be usable by anyone.

## The fastest path — use the AI Registration Bundle

The simplest way to produce a Mechanism Record is the AI Registration Bundle: a single file you paste into any AI chat (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or similar). The AI reads the bundle, asks for the name of the authority you want analyzed, and produces a completed Mechanism Record you can download.

**Three steps:**

1. Open the [AI Registration Bundle](AI-Registration-Bundle.md). Copy its entire contents.
2. Open an AI chat tool. Paste the bundle into a new conversation.
3. Tell the AI which authority you want analyzed (e.g., *"Cleveland, OH Police Department"*). Send.

The AI produces a downloadable Mechanism Record clearly labeled `TEST / NOT AN OFFICIAL REGISTRATION`.

For a fuller walk-through of the citizen inquiry process, see [How to run a citizen inquiry](05-Human-Testing-Guide.md).

## Two kinds of records you can file

The Bounded Power Framework supports two distinct citizen actions, plus one optional authority action.

**Citizen action — Mechanism Record.** A structural analysis of an authority. The framework's main use. Produces the kind of record described above. Either AI-assisted or written by hand.

**Citizen action — Violation Report.** A focused report that an authority did, failed to do, or threatened to do something that violates SCBP-04 or exceeds the authority's own declared bounds. Shorter than a Mechanism Record, focused on a single incident, filed using the [Violation Report Form](../02-Forms/02-Stop-Trigger.md).

**Authority action — Self-registration.** An authority can choose to file its own Mechanism Record under the framework as a public act of structural disclosure. The same form, the same rules, and the same standards apply as for citizen-produced Mechanism Records.

All three kinds of records live in the [public registry](../04-Registry/).

## What "structural review" means

When you fill out the Registration Form (whether by AI or by hand), you are evaluating:

- What powers does this authority exercise?
- Who depends on the authority? Can they realistically opt out?
- What harms can occur, and how severe?
- What stops the authority if it goes wrong?
- What does the authority depend on (vendors, contracts, infrastructure)?
- Is oversight genuinely independent?
- Can the authority expand without review?

The form has 13 sections that walk through these questions systematically.

---

## What SCBP-04 brings to the review

SCBP-04 is the framework's Structural Constitution — 81 rules describing how authority must behave to remain bounded. Every section of the Registration Form references specific SCBP-04 rules.

Pay particular attention to:

- **Reversibility** — Can decisions be undone?
- **Rollback** — Can the authority itself be rolled back?
- **Transparency** — Is the authority's operation visible?
- **Dependency risk** — Is the authority captured by any vendor, funder, or political actor?
- **Independent review** — Is there an oversight body genuinely independent of the authority?
- **Non-participant protection** — Are people who never opted in protected?
- **Expansion by reinterpretation** — Can the authority grow without affirmative renewal?
- **Unplug-ability** — Can the authority actually be stopped?

The Mechanism Record surfaces gaps in these areas as findings.

## Important rules for filling out records

1. **Use facts whenever possible.** Cite public sources where available.
2. **Mark `UNKNOWN` instead of inventing data.** Gaps are honest disclosures.
3. **Mark `ASSUMPTION:` when estimating.** Briefly explain the basis.
4. **Separate evidence from inference.** Both are valid; conflating them is not.
5. **Be fair even when critical.** Use the dull-truth register described in the [Registration Process Methodology](../05-Reference/REGISTRATION-PROCESS-METHODOLOGY.md). A defender and a critic of the authority should both call your prose factually accurate.
6. **Structural analysis matters more than rhetoric.** The framework's rules surface findings; you do not need to editorialize.

## Goal

The goal is not ideological agreement. The goal is to help anyone — citizens, journalists, researchers, advocates, and authorities themselves — produce structural analysis that surfaces how authority is actually shaped, where it can be stopped, and where it cannot.

A well-filled Mechanism Record is closer to the structural truth of an authority than most institutional disclosure ever gets — because the form asks questions institutions are never otherwise required to answer.
