# PROBLEM THIS SOLVES

Modern institutions accumulate power over time.

Common failure patterns include:

- expansion without explicit authorization  
- persistence beyond original scope  
- lack of meaningful external interruption  
- delayed or avoided accountability  
- inability to reverse harmful actions  

Existing systems rely on:

- trust in actors  
- procedural norms  
- reactive oversight  

These mechanisms fail under pressure.

This system addresses the structural problem:

How to design authority systems that cannot expand, persist, or operate beyond defined limits without being automatically constrained.

The goal is not to prevent all failure.

The goal is to make failure:

- detectable  
- containable  
- reversible
